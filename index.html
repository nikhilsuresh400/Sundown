WHO WE WORK WITH

As one of the world’s largest sportswear manufacturers, Adidas has a rich history spanning nearly seven decades. Driven by its mission to “Make Sport Equal,” striving to level the playing field for all communities through inclusive programs and sustainable practices. With a brand value of over $15 billion, Adidas generates revenue primarily from footwear (55%), followed by sportswear and fashion-oriented apparel (38%). The company's iconic three-stripe design, acquired from Finnish Karhu Sports in 1952, has become synonymous with athletic excellence and style.

The company’s focus on innovative products, such as the iconic Air Jordan sneakers, and strategic partnerships with top athletes and sports teams, have contributed to its success. Today, Nike’s purpose is to “move the world forward through the power of sport,” achieved through initiatives like leveling the playing field, promoting sustainability, and expanding access to sports for all. With a diverse range of products catering to various categories, including running, basketball, football, and training, Nike has become a staple in many people’s wardrobes, both on and off the field.

Founded in 1908 as the Converse Rubber Shoe Company in Malden, Massachusetts. Initially producing basketball shoes, the brand evolved into a fashion staple over the years. Today, Converse is a subsidiary of Nike, Inc., marketing, distributing, and licensing footwear, apparel, and accessories. With a rich history spanning over a century, Converse has maintained its relevance and appeal to a wide range of consumers through its strong marketing strategy. The brand’s iconic Chuck Taylor All Star sneakers, introduced in the 1920s, have become a cultural phenomenon, symbolizing self-expression and individuality.

Provides a range of services, including hunting expeditions, support for law enforcement, and specialized tourism. In the context of undercar services, Hunter fields the largest dedicated support force in the industry. Additionally, Hunter Industries is a global manufacturer of irrigation products, offering water- and energy-efficient solutions for residential, commercial, and agricultural applications. The company has a strong commitment to customer support and has been certified by ISO 9001:2015 and AS9100 Rev D.

The company specializes in technical apparel for mountaineering and Alpine sports, with a focus on obsessive, precise design and production. Arc’teryx is built on the principle of continuous evolution, striving for the ultimate balance of lightness, performance, and durability. Arc’teryx has undergone changes in ownership, being acquired by Salomon Group in 2001 and then Amer Sports in 2005, with Anta Sports becoming the majority owner in 2018.

Afterpay has become a leading player in the BNPL market, offering flexible payment options and promoting financial inclusion. Founded in Australia in 2014 by Nick Molnar and Anthony Eisen, the company has expanded its operations to the United Kingdom, Canada, the United States, and New Zealand. Afterpay allows customers to split purchases into four interest-free installments, with no fees or interest charged if payments are made on time. The company has partnered with over 6,500 merchants worldwide, including major retailers.
